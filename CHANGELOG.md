# Changelog

All notable changes to the Willingness-to-Pay Framework. Versions refer to the framework document, not to the repository tooling.

## v5.0

The first public release.

**Added**
- A demand-side **inhibitor** layer: the structural brakes that stop a purchase even when a driver is active (functional, psychological, economic).
- A **magnitude** layer: the six determinants of how much a buyer will pay, beyond whether they pay.
- A **dynamics** part: how the dominant driver shifts across the purchase journey, and how purchases feed back into future decisions.
- A **context** part: organizational (business-to-business) buying with the decision-making unit, plus cultural, situational, and segment modifiers.
- A **usability** layer to offset the larger size: five clusters, a decision tree, and a provisional (unvalidated) measurement instrument.
- An **interactive diagnostic** tool that runs the decision tree, bilingual.
- A full **Indonesian** translation kept in step with the English.
- **Falsifiable test predictions** in the boundaries section, upgrading the falsifiability discussion from acknowledgment to testable claims.

**Restored from v3** (dropped in v4)
- The four product filters (intensity, frequency, alternatives, economics).
- The proposal questions and the SCQA opening.
- The journey-stage amplifier map.
- The latent and manifest state distinction.
- The practitioner failure-modes table.

**Changed**
- Cleaned for public release: internal labels and classifications removed, a single author line set, prose aligned to a public writing standard.

## v4.0

- Added the Identity driver and its Canvas value-proposition category.
- Added the cognitive-bypass pathway.
- Added Boredom, Awe, and Gratitude as amplifiers; reclassified Nostalgia as dual-function.
- Qualified the claim that emotions cannot drive purchase, for low-involvement contexts.
- Added the overlapping-lenses qualification, persuasion-knowledge and reactance constraints, ethical guardrails, and cultural weighting.
- Corrected the Wrath mechanism to identity rather than anger.

## v3.0

- Established the five-layer demand-and-supply architecture: premise, twelve drivers, four amplifier categories, twelve value-proposition categories, and a twenty-four-tactic activation toolkit.
